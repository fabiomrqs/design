Context

As Lead Designer at Sony Music, I led a team responsible for creating digital products across different areas of the company. My role combined team management, design strategy definition, and hands-on contribution to high-impact projects.

In 2016, the music industry was undergoing its biggest transformation in decades: the shift from CDs to streaming.

This change forced Sony Music to rethink its processes, products, and the way it connects with audiences.

Within this context, I led the creation of a loyalty and gamification ecosystem designed to bring fans closer to artists, encourage music discovery, and increase consumption of the label’s catalog.

The Problem

One of the areas supported by the Design team was Marketing. Their main challenge was:

How to get people to discover the label’s new releases

Every week, between 40 and 120 new tracks were released. At the same time, users’ attention was increasingly fragmented across platforms like TikTok and Instagram, making it more and more expensive to promote each release through media investment.

Kickoff

To structure the project, I organized a Design Sprint with the Design team.

During the Discovery phase, we conducted interviews with stakeholders from Marketing, Engineering, Operations, and Spotify, and analyzed music consumption data alongside behavioral research such as “Culture Next”.

Key findings

  • New generations value musical diversity
  • Rewards drive behavior
  • Isolated rewards did not create emotional connection
  • Music accompanies users throughout the day
  • RConnecting digital and real-world experiences increases engagement

Building a vision

With the main insights in hand, we entered an exploration phase to understand how to turn music discovery into a truly engaging experience.

We explored different approaches, such as:

  • Cashback programs Made the relationship purely transactional
  • Traditional points systems Did not encourage recurring use
  • Curated editorial content Heavily dependent on human curation and limited at scale
  • Release-based promotional campaigns Generated attention spikes but no retention
  • Fan communities Complex to moderate and difficult to scale
  • Gamified experiences Created continuous incentives for discovery

At the end of this process, we converged on a single product vision.

Turning consumption into experience

The main mindset shift in the project was moving away from seeing streaming purely as a consumption metric. We started treating every play as an opportunity for connection between fans and artists.

Instead of focusing only on plays, we aimed to encourage music discovery in a more natural way.

Discovering and listening to music was already existing behavior. Rather than changing it, we decided to amplify it.

My vision was based on 4 principles:

  • Music discovery should be fun
  • Rewards must have real value
  • Every interaction should bring users closer to artists
  • Gamification should encourage habits, never feel like an obligation

Executive alignment

After consolidating the product vision, I prepared the strategic narrative and led presentations for Sony Music’s executive leadership.

The goal was to demonstrate how the solution could create value simultaneously for fans, artists, and the business.

The proposal was approved and became the strategic direction for platform development.

The gamification ecosystem

The solution evolved into a fully integrated loyalty program connected to Spotify.

Users linked their accounts and authorized access to their listening history.

Whenever they listened to tracks recommended by the platform, they earned coins that could be exchanged for real-world benefits such as concert tickets, official artist merchandise, electronics, and more.

More than a points program, the platform created a continuous cycle of music discovery.

Sustaining engagement

To turn recurring use into habit, we designed multiple gamification mechanics:

  • Daily missions
  • Collectible badges
  • Progression levels
  • Themed campaigns
  • Mystery rewards

Results

The platform quickly gained traction and became part of the promotional strategy for several Sony Music artists.

Beyond increasing music consumption, the product created a new channel for artist-fan engagement, reducing reliance on paid media investment to drive releases.

  • 400K+ active users
  • 300M+ streams generated
  • Cost per stream reduced from R$0.12 to R$0.03 compared to traditional media
  • 36 artists reached Spotify charts over two years

Key Learnings

This project showed that gamification is not about distributing points or rewards.

When used to reinforce natural user behaviors, it can create value for both users and the business.

It also reinforced the importance of validating hypotheses early, aligning different teams around a shared vision, and building products driven by behavior, not just features.
