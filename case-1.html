Context

Spring Global develops software for consumer goods companies, supporting everything from order capture to delivery execution and financial reconciliation. The platform is used by some of the largest brands in the industry, including Coca-Cola, Unilever, and Red Bull.

As a Staff Product Designer, I lead strategic initiatives focused on improving operational efficiency across the platform.

In this case study, I highlight a project that started as a delivery performance issue but ultimately revealed a much larger challenge within the payment process.

Finding the Root Cause

Every day, more than 250,000 deliveries are completed through Spring's platform. One of the most common concerns raised by our customers was the amount of time drivers were spending at stores during deliveries.

After analyzing operational data and interviewing drivers, a clear pattern emerged: what initially appeared to be a delivery problem was actually a payment problem. Payment collection was creating friction across the entire operation, impacting drivers, managers, and finance teams alike.

What initially appeared to be a delivery problem was actually a payment problem.

Shaping the Vision

With a clearer understanding of the problem, I brought together stakeholders from Product, Engineering, Operations, and Sales to explore different approaches to reducing payment friction.

Rather than redesigning the entire payment ecosystem, we focused on improving the existing workflow and delivering value more quickly.

I translated these ideas into high-fidelity concepts and prototypes, helping the team align on a clear direction for the solution.

Designing for Operational Efficiency

The guiding principle behind the solution was reducing payment friction without disrupting existing operations. Any solution needed to fit naturally into the delivery workflow while supporting the flexibility required by different payment methods and customer scenarios.

I focused on three core experiences: the driver experience in the field, the manager experience for monitoring revenue and operations, and the finance experience for payment reconciliation.

Through prototyping and usability testing with drivers, I refined these concepts and incorporated key learnings into the final solution.

Results

The impact of the new payment experience extended far beyond the delivery workflow. By reducing payment friction, the solution improved operational efficiency across drivers, managers, and finance teams.

Some key impact metrics so far:

  • 52% reduction in payment processing time
  • 92% reduction in post-payment verification
  • Nearly 100% real-time transaction visibility
  • 24% increase in daily route capacity per driver
  • Reduced operational costs associated with payment reconciliation
  • Scalable foundation for future payment products
